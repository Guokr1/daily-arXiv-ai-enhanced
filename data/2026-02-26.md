Abstract: The far side of the Moon, shielded from terrestrial radio frequency interference and beyond the influence of Earth's ionosphere, should offer a uniquely quiet environment for radio astronomy and cosmological experiments. Achieving precision measurements in this band requires accurate understanding of antenna performance and systematics.
