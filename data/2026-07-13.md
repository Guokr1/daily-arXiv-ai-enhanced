Motivation: 为了描述单层OsC和RuC的电子与拓扑性质，需要构建一个低能有效的k·p模型，以便在保持物理可解释性的同时准确再现第一性原理计算结果。

Method: 基于第一性原理计算验证动力学稳定性与拓扑非平庸的Z₂不变量；利用Γ点对称性分析构建多带k·p哈密顿量，通过Löwdin分区约化为四带低能模型；拟合参数以匹配ab initio能带结构。

Result: 得到块对角形式的有效哈密顿量，两个块由时间反演对称性关联，与BHZ模型类似但包含二次动量依赖的对称允许非对角耦合项，成功拟合了低能区域的能带结构。

Conclusion: 构建的紧凑模型能够有效分析单层OsC和RuC的电子与拓扑性质，其非标准BHZ形式的耦合项修正了Γ点附近的低能色散关系。

Abstract: We derive a low-energy $\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{p}$ effective Hamiltonian for monolayer osmium carbide (OsC) and ruthenium carbide (RuC) in a planar hexagonal configuration. First-principles calculations indicate that both monolayers are dynamically stable and exhibit features of a two-dimensional quantum spin Hall (QSH) phase, characterized by a nontrivial $\mathbb{Z}_2$ topological invariant. Using symmetry analysis at the $Γ$ point, we construct a multiband $\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{p}$ Hamiltonian including spin-orbit coupling and reduce it to a four-band low-energy model through Löwdin partitioning. The effective Hamiltonian has a block-diagonal form, with two blocks related by time-reversal symmetry, analogous to the Bernevig--Hughes--Zhang (BHZ) model. In contrast to the standard BHZ form, the symmetry-allowed off-diagonal coupling contains quadratic momentum-dependent terms, which modify the low-energy dispersion near the $Γ$ point. The fitted parameters reproduce the ab initio band structures in the low-energy region, yielding a compact model for analyzing the electronic and topological properties of monolayer OsC and RuC.
