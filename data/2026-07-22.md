Method: 开发了PDS1500单机箱64通道读出系统，采用1 GSa/s实时采样、14-bit ADC（ENOB 10.6）、64 Gbps总吞吐量架构，并设计了确定性零延迟时钟分布方案。通过台架测试和在升级后的JNE-1ton原型探测器上的部署进行验证，并以商用参考系统为基准进行性能对比评估。

Result: 所有关键指标均满足JNE实验要求：1000 ns采集窗口内零数据丢失；基线噪声降至参考系统水平的1/3；跨电源周期定时漂移限制在0.3 ns以内；能量阈值低至0.1 MeV，可探测低能太阳中微子。14-bit物理分辨率提供了更高的波形保真度，但整体能量分辨率仍受限于JNE-1ton探测器本身的固有性能。模块化架构支持扩展至3000通道全规模探测器。

Conclusion: 新开发的电子学系统完全满足未来JNE实验的技术要求，为锦屏中微子实验的高精度波形重建和低能中微子探测提供了可靠的读出解决方案。

Abstract: The Jinping Neutrino Experiment (JNE), a next-generation neutrino observatory under construction at the China Jinping Underground Laboratory II (CJPL-II), requires high-precision waveform-based event reconstruction, imposing stringent demands on its readout electronics. To meet these requirements, we have developed a high-performance readout system featuring 1 GSa/s real-time sampling, 14-bit physical resolution with an effective number of bits (ENOB) of 10.6, a total data throughput of 64 Gbps, and a deterministic zero-delay clock distribution architecture. The new single-crate 64-channel system (PDS1500) was validated through bench tests and deployment on the upgraded JNE-1ton prototype detector. Its performance was further evaluated against a commercial reference system. The results demonstrate that all key metrics meet the JNE experimental requirements: zero data loss within a 1000 ns acquisition window, baseline noise reduced to one-third of the reference level, timing drift limited to 0.3 ns across power cycles, and an energy threshold as low as 0.1 MeV, enabling the detection of low-energy solar neutrinos. While the 14-bit physical resolution provides significantly higher waveform fidelity, the overall energy resolution in this test remains dominated by the intrinsic limitations of the JNE-1ton detector, as expected. Furthermore, the modular architecture provides the throughput and scalability required to support the full-scale 3000-channel JNE detector. These results collectively demonstrate that the newly developed electronics system fully satisfies the technical requirements of the future JNE experiment.
